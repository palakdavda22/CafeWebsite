Burgers and Wraps That Are Made For India

Today, it won’t be an exaggeration to say that burgers and wraps have revolutionized the food industry. We don’t want to go through the trouble of dealing with curries and accompaniments, that’s just a hassle – we want our food in easily consumable, and rightly so. Our hectic lifestyle calls for less mess on our plates.



Thankfully, burgers and wraps have solved this problem for us. With each bite, we get small dosages of all our nutrient needs – carbohydrates from the wheat and bread, protein from the patty, and vitamins from the veggies. You may be working on your laptop or are on the go to meet someone, these two items make sure you have a filling meal without all the frill.



And when we think of burgers and wraps, especially burgers, the top name that comes to most minds is McDonald’s. And why not, after all they were the first to offer India a taste of this millennial’s favourite food item.



Ever since its entry into Indian markets in 1996, McDonald’s has played a definitive role in molding the eating habits of today’s millennials. Not just the taste, the McDonald’s burger price has also been approved by patrons over the years. After burgers, they also launched iconic wraps that became instant hits and are easily available burger online. Let’s take a look at some burgers and wraps which, owing to their awesomeness, have found a permanent place in the McDonald’s menu.



McAloo Tikki Burger

The first original ‘Indian Burger’, this innovative McDonald’s menu item has been an ever present feature of McDonald’s India (South & West) menu ever since the chain was first established in the country. Almost two decades later and counting, even today, a McDonald’s burger means McAloo Tikki burger for many. The McAloo Tikki burger was curated by McDonald’s chefs who wanted Indian customers to feel a sense of familiarity when they walk into McDonald’s for the first time ever with pocket friendly McAloo Tikki prices. What better way to achieve that response than by encasing one of India’s iconic snacks, the aloo tikki in between two buns? The rest, as they say, is history.



Chicken Maharaja Mac and veg Maharaja Mac Burgers

The Maharaja Mac is McDonald’s India – South and West’s answer to the Big Mac. This double decker burger is a sight to behold, isn’t it? Ever since it was introduced in the earliest McDonald’s outlets in India, it has cemented its place in the McDonald’s menu, and in the hearts of whoever has gorged on one of these. Complete with jalapenos, iceberg lettuce, tomatoes, onions, cheddar cheese and McDonald’s sauces to go with two layers of patties, this burger delivers a truly wholesome meal. And it is not just the name, the taste of Chicken Maharaja Mac and Veg Maharaja Mac is fit for the big Indian hearts as well. McSpicy Chicken and McSpicy Paneer Burgers

Spices are an important part of the Indian culinary arts. Naturally, we have developed an affinity for spicy foods. With the McSpicy Chicken and McSpicy Paneer burger, McDonald’s has crafted something that very deftly finds the balance between the Eastern and Western cuisines. Although in the shape of and with all the properties of a burger, the McSpicy Chicken burger and the McSpicy Paneer Burger have a taste which is essentially Indian. One of the highlights of this McDonald’s burger is the crispy coated patty which is soft and succulent on the inside.



Big Spicy Chicken and Big Spicy Paneer Wraps

The Big Spicy Wrap recently went through a makeover, one of the best burger with adjustments made to its size and level of spiciness. The new and improved Big Spicy Chicken and Big Spicy Paneer Wrap retain the component that made them popular in the first place, the crisp patty. The whole wheat wrap complements the filling, and there are several health benefits associated with whole wheat products, and is far healthier than ‘maida’ alternatives. McDonald’s wraps have their own touch of Indian kitchens, thanks to the array of spices that give it its unique and distinctive taste. These are hands down the best wraps you will find.
